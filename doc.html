2. Dashboard Risk Management & Setup Metrics

The top-right quadrant of your Copier Dashboard features a dynamic, real-time Risk Projection Matrix. This engine displays your active sizing parameters and instantly calculates your maximum capital exposure before you fire your ladder.

  • ANCHOR COUNT: The number of initial market orders executed immediately at the current market price. (e.g., an Anchor Count of 3 will fire 3 separate positions upon execution).
  • ANCHOR LOT: The specific lot size assigned individually to each market (Anchor) order.
  • PENDING COUNT: The number of pending limit orders placed at each individual tier down the ladder grid. (If set to 2, the EA places a twin-order block at every grid step).
  • PENDING LOT: The lot size assigned individually to each pending limit order.
  • LAYER COUNT: Sets the total depth of the ladder (number of limit grid tiers) generated down the chart. A Layer Count of 5 will generate 5 distinct pending levels spaced at your configured pip-step intervals.
  • STOP LOSS (PIPS): The protective distance (in pips) for the entire grid family, measured relative to the primary anchor entry price.
  • MAX RISK CALCULATOR: A dynamic, real-time math engine projecting your worst-case total loss scenario:
  • The Assessment: The EA instantly calculates the exact dollar and percentage loss if your initial Anchors are hit, AND every single configured Pending Layer is 100% filled, before the entire family hits your Stop Loss.
  • Dynamic Colour Warning: Displays in Green (SAFE) for conservative exposure (under 2% of balance), scaling into Amber (CAUTION) at 2–5%, and Red (DANGER) at 5%+, ensuring you maintain ultimate oversight of your account margin.
  • R:R Ratio: Displays the Reward-to-Risk ratio for the configured TP/SL setup (e.g., R:R 1:3.2 $230.40). Shows TP/SL OFF when TP mode is disabled. The dollar figure represents the maximum potential reward at the deepest TP target.

How Stealth Mode Works

When TP/SL STEALTH is active, the EA keeps your real SL and TP targets entirely in its own memory — invisible to your broker. This prevents stop-hunting and spread manipulation.

  • Virtual Targets: The true, narrow SL and TP targets are stored locally in MT5 Global Variables (GSAE_VSL_ and GSAE_VTP_), not on the broker's server.
  • Broker Sees Nothing: Orders are placed with TP = 0 and (optionally) a wide Emergency SL as a safety net.
  • Real-Time Monitoring: The EA's StealthMonitor engine checks every 200ms. When price touches your virtual TP or SL, the EA instantly closes or partially closes the position.
  • All Features Work: Trailing stop, break-even, harvest, and manual SL/TP adjustments all update the virtual targets locally — nothing is sent to the broker.

Emergency SL Safety Net

When Stealth Mode is active, you can optionally place an Emergency Stop Loss on the broker as a safety net. This protects your account if the EA loses connection (terminal crash, internet outage, power failure).

6. Harvesting & Adaptive Profit Defense

Controlled via the HARVEST ON / OFF toggle button, Harvesting is an intelligent, automated risk-defence engine designed specifically for active Grid entries.

  • Objective: Secures profits on upper trade tiers during deep market pullbacks, allowing your portfolio to recover capital incrementally.
  • How it Works: When the price dives deep enough to trigger discount limit orders, the engine steps in to protect your higher-priced entry tiers. It automatically moves the Stop Loss of previous upper tiers to their own entry prices (+1.0 pip cushion).
  • The Advantage: On any minor market bounce, your upper "underwater" tiers are instantly liquidated at 100% Break-Even, freeing up your margin while leaving your deepest discount tier active to run all the way to its full profit target!
  • Threshold Guard: Uses a mathematical safety threshold (at least 50% of your grid step distance) to ensure that minor spread noise or slippage never triggers a harvest prematurely.

How Harvest Interacts with Break-Even

When Harvest is ON, the EA forces Break-Even reference mode to ENTRY regardless of your BE MODE setting. This ensures that each tier's SL moves to its own entry price (not the shared anchor), which is critical for the harvest logic to work correctly.

Trigger Sequence:

  • The EA waits for price to reach the configured Trigger BE level (e.g., TP1 or TP2).
  • Once triggered, the Break-Even engine moves the SL of all qualifying trades to their entry price + 1.0 pip (configurable via InpHarvestPips).
  • Upper tiers that are underwater are now locked at break-even — they close on the smallest bounce.
  • The deepest discount tier remains active with its original TP target, running for maximum profit.

🧬 High-IQ Institutional Shave Architecture

To prevent the standard industry trap of "shrinking residuals" (where EAs calculate percentages on shrinking remainders, leaving microscopic open trades indefinitely), the Gold Scalper EA implements an institutional-grade, self-correcting scaling algorithm:

  • Initial Entry Caching: The moment your ticket opens, the EA mathematically "memorizes" the Original Opening Lot Volume (e.g., 0.50 Lots) inside its persistent cache memory.
  • Cumulative Target Grid: At every TP level, the engine computes the ideal Cumulative Remaining Volume rather than isolated closed amounts. (For 0.50 lots @ 20% increments: TP1 target is 0.40 lots, TP2 target is 0.30 lots).
  • Self-Correcting Cuts: The EA calculates the exact gap between the *active live volume* and the *ideal target volume* at the next trigger. This mathematically compensates for any broker slippage, spread drift, or manual micro-adjustments, ensuring a guaranteed perfect liquidation at your final target!

9. Zone Execution Mode (Signal Zone vs EA Settings)

When a signal provider sends a price zone (e.g., BUY @ 4500-4503), the EA can handle it in two ways:

  • EA Settings (Default): The EA uses its own Pending Count and Ladder Step settings. The zone top becomes a single entry price, and pending orders are placed using your configured spacing. This gives you full control over grid density regardless of the signal format.
  • Signal Zone: The zone geometry overrides your Pending Count. The EA calculates the number of pending orders based on zone width divided by your configured Ladder Step, then places them using your channel's Ladder Step spacing. For example, a zone of 4500-4560 with a 30-pip Ladder Step generates 2 pending orders spaced 30 pips apart.

Configure this per channel in the EA input parameters: InpCx_RespectZone (default: false = EA Settings).

11. Trailing Stop Engine

The EA features an automated Trailing Stop Engine that progressively locks in profit by moving the Stop Loss closer to the current price as the trade moves in your favour. It operates independently from Break-Even and only activates after Break-Even has already been secured (SL at or past entry).

Trail Modes

Cycle through modes via the TRAIL button on the Copier or Manual panel (TRAIL: OFF → TRAIL: STEP → TRAIL: CANDLE → TRAIL: FRACTAL):

ModeBehaviourBest For
STEPSL follows price at a fixed distance behind it, moving in discrete increments. Uses InpTrailDistance (default 20 pips) and InpTrailStep (default 5 pips).Trending markets with steady momentum
CANDLESL is placed behind the previous closed M15 candle's Low (BUY) or High (SELL) + optional padding (InpTrailPadding).Structure-based traders who trust candle wicks
FRACTALSL is placed behind the lowest Low / highest High of the last 5 M15 bars + optional padding. Provides a wider, more conservative trail.Conservative trailing with swing structure protection

How Each Mode Works

STEP Mode (Default):

  • Calculates a theoretical SL at Current Price - InpTrailDistance (BUY) or Current Price + InpTrailDistance (SELL).
  • Only moves the SL if the new level is at least InpTrailStep pips above (BUY) or below (SELL) the current SL.
  • This prevents micro-adjustments on every tick while still locking in profit during strong moves.

CANDLE Mode:

  • Reads the previous closed M15 candle (index [0] = most recently closed bar).
  • For BUY: places SL at M15 Low - InpTrailPadding.
  • For SELL: places SL at M15 High + InpTrailPadding.
  • Only moves SL toward profit (never backward).

FRACTAL Mode:

  • Scans the last 5 M15 bars to find the structural swing point.
  • For BUY: places SL at Lowest Low of 5 bars - InpTrailPadding.
  • For SELL: places SL at Highest High of 5 bars + InpTrailPadding.
  • Provides the widest trail of all modes — ideal for catching major trend moves.

Key Rules

  • BE-First: The trail engine is dormant until Break-Even is secured (SL already at or past entry). This prevents premature trailing on entries that haven't reached safety yet.
  • One-Way Only: SL only moves toward profit. Once the trail sets a level, it never reverts.
  • Per-Channel: C1 and C2 have independent trail mode settings. Manual trades use the manual trail mode.
  • Stealth Compatible: In Stealth TP mode, the trail engine uses the virtual SL (not the broker SL) for comparisons.

21. Advanced Guide: Trade Exclusions, Command Routing & Copier Workflow

This section provides an advanced, user-friendly breakdown of how the Copier Bridge and the Gold Scalper EA interact under the hood, and how to surgically customise trade exclusions, keywords, and automated remote commands.

🔄 The End-to-End Copier Workflow

To help you understand exactly how your signals are copied from Telegram to MetaTrader 5, here is the lifecycle of a copied trade:

  • Signal Broadcast: A signal provider posts a message in your subscribed Telegram Channel (e.g., "BUY XAUUSD @ 2030, SL: 2020, TP1: 2035, TP2: 2040").
  • Bridge Parsing: The Copier Bridge Desktop Engine instantly intercepts the message, processes it through the Strict Anti-Noise Filters, and extracts the entry price, Stop Loss, and Take Profits.
  • Secure Delivery: The Bridge delivers the parsed signal to the EA via TCP socket IPC (~50ms latency). As a fallback, it also writes a .txt signal file inside the secure shared MetaQuotes Common directory.
  • EA Detection & Context Routing: The MT5 EA immediately receives the signal, matches the Telegram Channel ID to your preset tab (CH1 or CH2), and extracts the trade context.
  • Filters & Cutoffs Evaluation: Before execution, the EA verifies:
  • Is the signal older than 60 seconds? (If yes, discarded).
  • Does it contain any configured Trade Exclusions? (If yes, ignored).
  • Is the current market price within the Entry Cutoff range? (If yes, executed).
  • Instant Execution: The EA fires the anchor trades and pending limit grid precisely matching your channel settings (CH1 or CH2 lot size, steps, and take profits).

🛡️ How SL & TP Are Applied

The EA always uses your channel settings for Stop Loss and Take Profit — not the signal provider's values. This keeps you protected if a provider sends incorrect or dangerously wide targets.

  • Stop Loss: Always uses your channel's SL pip setting (e.g. 60 pips). Signal SL prices are ignored. Your stop is always calculated relative to your anchor entry price.
  • Take Profit: Signal TPs are only used when they are in the correct direction (above entry for BUY, below entry for SELL). If a provider sends TPs in the wrong direction (e.g. below market for a BUY), the EA falls back to your channel's TP presets automatically.
  • Ladder Step & Pending Count: When a signal contains a price zone (e.g. @ 4500-4503), the EA uses your channel's Ladder Step to space pending orders. The zone only determines how many pending levels are placed.

🚫 How to Configure Trade Exclusions (Keyword Blocking)

You can configure Trade Exclusions to prevent specific setups from firing. This is highly useful if a channel provider posts a setup you want to ignore, or posts standard chat messages that look like trades.

  • Exclusion Keywords Box: Located in your Copier UI under the Parsing Settings tab.
  • How to Add Exclusions: Enter comma-separated keywords into the Exclusions text box (e.g. pending, test, limit, break, demo).
  • Surgical Filtering: If an incoming message contains any of your configured exclusion keywords, the Bridge instantly blocks the signal. The EA will not open any trades, and the Copier log footer will record: "Signal skipped due to exclusion keyword filter."

💻 Automated Remote Commands (Conversational Triggers)

The Copier Bridge does not just fire new setups; it listens for active updates from channel leaders to automatically protect your open positions:

  • Breakeven Commands (CMD,RISK_FREE):
  • Triggers: Channel updates containing terms like risk free, secure profit, move sl to be, break even, lock profits, sl to entry, or secure now.
  • Action: The Copier Bridge translates this into a high-speed command. The MT5 EA immediately moves the Stop Loss of all active trades in that channel's family to their entry price plus a safety spread buffer, instantly locking in safety.
  • Close All Commands (CMD,CLOSE_ALL):
  • Triggers: Channel updates containing terms like close all, close now, exit trade, cancel setup, close gold, i am out, or exit now.
  • Action: The Copier Bridge immediately signals a total flat command. The MT5 EA forcefully liquidates all open positions and deletes all pending limit grid levels for that specific channel's family, securing your cash profits immediately.
